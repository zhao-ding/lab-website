Zhao, Ding

Assistant Professor| Carnegie Mellon University

Ding Zhao is an Assistant Professor of the Department of Mechanical Engineering with affiliation positions at the Robotic Institute and the Wilton E. Scott Institute for Energy Innovation at the Carnegie Mellon University. Directing the Safe AI Lab at CMU, Professor Zhao aims to develop safe, transparent, and good-for-all AI with mathematically provable performance in the face of the real-world, uncertain, dynamic, multiple agents, and possibly human-involved environment by bridging statistics and cybernetics.

Professor Zhao is recognized nationally and internationally for his research on autonomous/connected vehicles and smart cities. His work is featured in the New York Times, Forbes, Futurism, and Automotive News. He has worked with several companies, including Uber, Ford, and Toyota as well as the Department of Energy.

Recruiting:

I am currently recruiting PhD students, visiting PhD students, master students, and senior undergraduates. Here are answers to some commonly asked questions.

  • I plan to recruit 2~3 new PhDs in the 2019 fall.
  • I usually do not work with students who are not CMU on campus students during the fall and winter semesters.
  • Funding is usually not available if you already get credits for doing research. Funding in the summer is possible, with priority to those who have been working with me during the previous semesters. Exceptions can be made if you are exceptionally productive.
  • If you are applying for a visiting position from a foreign country, you need to contact me FOUR months earlier than you plan to arrive. I may not be able to send the inviting letters and I2019 in time if you contact me later than that due to some university rules.
If you are interested in collaborating with me, please fill in this form to apply. It requires your CV and a 3-slide PPT describing your previous research experience. You can also directly send emails to me, but I may need more time to evaluate your case than using the linked form.
